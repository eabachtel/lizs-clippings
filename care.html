Plant Care Tips

Real life care for real life plants. Nothing fancy, just what works.

Pothos

Light: Bright indirect to low light. Avoid strong direct sun on leaves.

Water: Let the top 1 to 2 inches of soil dry before watering. Leaves drooping a bit often means it is time.

Notes: Very forgiving. Trim vines to keep it full, and root cuttings in water or soil to create new pots.

Snake Plant

Light: Low to bright indirect light. Handles low light well.

Water: Water sparingly. Let soil dry almost completely. In winter, water even less often.

Notes: Great for forgetful waterers. Use a well draining soil and a pot with drainage holes.

Spider Plant

Light: Bright indirect light for best growth.

Water: Keep soil lightly moist but not soggy. Let the top of the soil dry slightly between waterings.

Notes: Produces baby plants that can be potted up once they form small roots.

ZZ Plant

Light: Low to medium indirect light. Avoid full direct sun.

Water: Water when the soil is fully dry. Thick roots and stems store water.

Notes: Great low maintenance plant. Use caution with pets and kids who nibble on leaves.
